Technology Choices

In the previous page, the risks of electronic voting machines have been described. This page will demonstrate the possible solutions to prevent some of the problems.

The first risk given is that the software of the voting machines could be under cyber-attacks. The problem could be prevented in several ways. The first method is securing databases and networks. The risk of cyber-attack can be minimized by setting firewalls and encryption of data. Firewall will track the traffic between the machine and the internet. Keeping the voting software on all the machines up to date is also important. The older version of software is more vulnerable and prone to cyber-attacks. Installation of security software will help prevent cyber-attacks. The software should include anti-virus and anti-spyware. The machine companies can ask independent computer science experts to test the programme and give feedback or hire ‘white hats’ to find out the vulnerabilities of the programme and fix them before the attacks actually happen. White hats refer to ethical hackers who are motivated to identify vulnerabilities of security and inform them to the manufacturer or the client organization. In order to prevent the physical attacks to the hardware, cameras or alarms can be installed at the polling spaces or guards can be hired during the vote seasons to keep the machines.

The concerns regarding inoperative machines could be simply sorted by having another machine for back up. It is recommended to have at least two machines at the same place so the vote can still proceed in case one of the machines does not work. There would be not only old generations but also young people struggling with how the electronic voting machines work. Therefore, There should be trained staff at the polling places to show people how to use the voting machines. People who live in rural areas may need to travel a long distance, however, portable electricity generators can come with the voting machines to save time.

The voting system can be modified to require only minimum personal details of voters to minimize the risk of privacy problems. Also, personal details can be converted into a secret code using encryption and it will help prevent crimes such as data theft. Implementing multi factor authentication will add another layer of security to keep the personal information safe. Perhaps, people can receive verification code to enter the system through texts or emails and this way there will be less concerns regarding privacy issues. Pre-election testing on all the voting machines can be conducted prior to the actual election to ensure that all the machines are in good working conditions. Also, it has to be checked if the correct voting programme is installed on the machines and the programme performs properly.
